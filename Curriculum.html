Our School's STEM Pathways

Technology has become all the more relevant. Thus, our school offers three STEM pathways in attempts to expose students to the work environment and help them become more familiar. At our school, we have five different pathways that students are able to choose from when entering high school. The pathways are global leadership and business as well as three STEM related pathways: computer science, engineering, and biotechnology.

The purpose of these pathways are to help students follow their passions and explore their interests in a specific field. With STEM specifically, students can choose from three pathways available: Computer Science, Engineering, and Biotechnology. The Computer Science pathway allows students to experience a wide range of computer science related interests, from coding and website design to robotics. The biotechnology pathway allows students to investigate the effects of technology on health and life sciences as well as participating in experiments to further their knowledge of the sciences. The Engineering pathway allows students to participate in a program called Project Lead The Way. Here, students learn about the different techniques of engineering and how to design their own prototypes to solve everyday problems.

However, students aren't restricted to these STEM related pathways as they have the option of taking either Global Leadership or Business. The Global Leadership Pathway educates students about the meaning of leadership and teaches students on how to collaborate with others in a group. This pathway examines current conflicts and problems all around the world so as to broaden the students persepectives and world views. Finally, the business pathway provides students with an understanding practical business skills. Students work on various elements of business and study all aspects that pertain to business. In this pathway, students have the opportunity to compete in a competition called Business Professionals of America (BPA).

Our school, through these pathways, allow students to experience all aspects of a certain career field so as to prepare them for the real world. The modern world is fast-paced and growing continuously. With vast improvements in technology and new innovations being developed everyday, science and with technology. This will ensure that the future generations will be able to find jobs in a rapidly changing economy.


Program Courses Description

Computer Science
  1. Exploring Computer Science
  2. AP - Computer Science Principles
  3. AP Computer Science A
 The computer science pathway helps students develop computational practices of algorithm development, problem solving, and programming related to many topics. Students will also create web pages and design and code test programs using Python. In addition, students learn how to create apps and learn the basic fundamentals of robotic design.

Engineering
  1. PLTW Intro to Engineering Design
  2. PLTW Principles of Engineering
  3. PLTW Engineering Design/ Development
 The engineering pathway follows a program known as Project Lead the Way (PLTW) which introduces students to different topics and techniques engineers use in their different fields. The first two years of this pathway focus on learning the different skills of the engineering process while the third level is a year-long project where the students make an invention or innovation to a current problem in their lives or society.

Biotechnology
  1. Fund of Biotech & Health Science
  2. Essentials to Biotech R&D
  3. Advance Biotechnology & Health Care
 Students in the biotechnology pathway practice hands-on laboratory experiments. While implementing technology in their everyday class, students learn how scientists use technological techniques to further their investigations including equipment like pipets, laminar hoods, and incubators.

Business
  1. Business, Finance, and Marketing I
  2. Marketing, Management, and Communications II
  3. Marketing, Management, and Communications III
Students learn the basis for life-long practical business for the business pathway. In addition to working on the elements of business such as business administration, entrepreneurship, financial analysis, and marketing, students also study various business aspects, such as business law, economic principles, ethics, entrepreneurship, financial analysis, marketing operations, and management plans.
