Program Courses Description

Computer Science
  1. Exploring Computer Science
  2. AP - Computer Science Principles
  3. AP Computer Science A
 The computer science pathway helps students develop computational practices of algorithm development, problem solving, and programming related to many topics. Students will also create web pages and design and code test programs using Python. In addition, students learn how to create apps and learn the basic fundamentals of robotic design.

Engineering
  1. PLTW Intro to Engineering Design
  2. PLTW Principles of Engineering
  3. PLTW Engineering Design/ Development
 The engineering pathway follows a program known as Project Lead the Way (PLTW) which introduces students to different topics and techniques engineers use in their different fields. The first two years of this pathway focus on learning the different skills of the engineering process while the third level is a year-long project where the students make an invention or innovation to a current problem in their lives or society.

Biotechnology
  1. Fund of Biotech & Health Science
  2. Essentials to Biotech R&D
  3. Advance Biotechnology & Health Care
 Students in the biotechnology pathway practice hands-on laboratory experiments. While implementing technology in their everyday class, students learn how scientists use technological techniques to further their investigations including equipment like pipets, laminar hoods, and incubators.

Business
  1. Business, Finance, and Marketing I
  2. Marketing, Management, and Communications II
  3. Marketing, Management, and Communications III
Students learn the basis for life-long practical business for the business pathway. In addition to working on the elements of business such as business administration, entrepreneurship, financial analysis, and marketing, students also study various business aspects, such as business law, economic principles, ethics, entrepreneurship, financial analysis, marketing operations, and management plans.
